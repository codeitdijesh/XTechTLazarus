EdgeCuda Plan Visualization

Recursive ZK Tactical Swarm

A planning-level architecture diagram for a BitTorrent-over-MANET swarm secured by lightweight recursive proofs and visualized through a future Kepler.gl commander map.

Recommended Proof Track

Plonky2 recursion

Use Plonky2 for recursive aggregation. Keep Groth16 as a leaf-proof comparison path, not the primary recursive architecture.

Lane 01

Tactical Data Flow

Commander Node Owns the approved mission payload and publishes the Merkle root.
↓
Chunking Layer Splits the 10 GB file into chunks and hashes leaves into a Merkle tree.
↓
BitTorrent-over-MANET Drones trade missing chunks with local peers instead of all pulling from command.
↓
Bandwidth Delta Commander avoids repeated full-file broadcast and sends only seed data plus proof traffic.

Lane 02

Recursive Proof Path

Leaf Chunk Proof Each drone proves chunk membership against the commander-approved root.
↓
Pairwise Aggregation Peers verify and combine proof states in a recursive tree.
↓
Final Swarm Proof The commander receives one aggregate proof for the verified swarm state.
Invalid proof exits aggregation path
Quarantine Decision Bad chunks, spoofed roots, or malformed proofs remove the node from routing.

Lane 03

Commander Visualization

Kepler.gl Map Primary geospatial layer for drone positions, routes, trust state, and time playback.
↓
Swarm Dataset Rows include node id, lat/lon, route id, chunk count, proof status, and trust state.
↓
Metrics Overlay Shows bandwidth saved, verified nodes, quarantined nodes, and aggregation depth.
↓
Quarantine Overlay Failed nodes turn red and disappear from active P2P route calculations.
